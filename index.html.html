Programme contents

8 weeks. Everything you need.
Nothing you already know.

The programme is built specifically for certified functional safety engineers. It starts where your existing knowledge ends — at the boundary of process and nuclear.

01

8-week live online programme

Structured weekly content built for nuclear project environments. Not process plants, not textbooks. Practical and applied, built around what nuclear projects actually need from a functional safety engineer. Live sessions — not recorded content you'll never finish.

02

Weekly live group calls — small cohort only

Live sessions where you show up, ask what you need to ask, and work through real scenarios with a small group of engineers on the same journey. The cohort is kept deliberately small — 6 engineers maximum — so every question gets proper attention, not a group chat reply.

03

Personal case study with written feedback

Not a multiple-choice quiz. A realistic nuclear functional safety scenario that you work through and submit — reviewed personally and returned with written feedback from Richard. This is how you know your thinking is sound before you walk into your first nuclear role.

04

3 months post-placement mentoring

When you land your first nuclear role — and you will — you could be the only functional safety engineer on the project. Monthly check-in calls for your first three months. Someone experienced in your corner when the grey areas appear and the decisions are hard.

05

Nuclear-specific templates and reference materials

Everything mapped to ONR guidance and nuclear-specific standards. Ready to use from day one of your new role — not something you have to build yourself under pressure in a new environment.

Common questions
I have my TÜV certification but no nuclear experience at all. Is this still for me?
Yes — that is exactly who this programme is designed for. Your TÜV certification tells Richard you understand the standards. This programme covers everything that sits on top of that in a nuclear context. Zero nuclear experience is the starting assumption.
How much time does the programme require each week?
Plan for 3–4 hours per week across the 8 weeks — one live group call plus structured content and reading. The case study requires a more focused block of time, but it is a single submission, not ongoing work.
What if I don't land a nuclear role within the 8 weeks?
Most engineers take 2–6 months to land their first nuclear role after completing the programme. The post-placement mentoring begins when you're in role — not when the programme ends. Richard stays in contact between the programme finishing and your placement.
Is this recorded so I can go back to it?
Live sessions are recorded for cohort members. All materials — templates, reference documents, case study feedback — remain accessible permanently. The live format matters because the interaction is where most of the learning happens, but the recordings are always there.
Will there be another cohort if I miss this one?
There will be future cohorts, but the founding cohort terms — price and the level of personal attention from Richard — will not be repeated. The founding cohort is small by design and the experience reflects that.
